Bias Reduction: Clipped Double Q VS. Multi-Step Method

Final Project for CSCI-SHU 375 reinforcement Learning

In actor-critic setting, Twin Delayed Deep Deterministic policy gradient algorithm (TD3) decelerates the overestimation bias by introducing a clipped variant of Double Q-leaning and achieves better results than Deep Deterministic Policy Gradient (DDPG). Another method proposed for bias reduction is Multi-step DDPG (MDDPG) which has comparable performance and is more computationally efficient than TD3. Our method builds on top of Soft algorithm Actor-Critic (SAC), which incorporates the clipped double Q target estimates and entropy maximization. We propose Multi-step SAC with Single Q target estimate (MSAC-Single) for our empirical studies on the effect of removing clipped double Q estimates from SAC and whether we can use multi-step method instead to get the same performance. Additionally, we also examine the performance of multi-step SAC (MSAC) with both bias reduction methods and make run time comparisons.

Performances of SAC, 5-step SAC (MSAC(5)), SAC with Single Q target estimate (SAC-Single),
5-step SAC with Single Q target estimate (MSAC-Single(5)).


SAC compared to MSAC with different step length (n=2, 5, 8) in terms of performance.

Overestimated value function has been identified as a critical problem in actor-critic methods. In this project, we empirically study the effectiveness of two bias reduction techniques: clipped double Q and multi-step methods by proposing two SAC variants, Multi-step Soft Actor-Critic with Single Q target estimate (MSAC-Single) and Multi-step Soft Actor-Critic (MSAC). We provide experimental evidence on the effects of removing the clipped double Q method and replacing it with multi-step method using MSAC-Single, which replaces the second Q target estimate with multi-step bootstrapping. Additionally, we examine the performance of MSAC with both the clipped variant of Double Q-learning and the multi-step methods. Results show that removing the clipped double Q component from SAC leads to a significant performance drop. Applying multi-step methods to SAC helps to alleviate the overestimation bias and improve performance, but it is unable to achieve the same result as the clipped double Q method. If we apply the multi-step method to SAC with clipped double Q target estimates, the performances decrease compared to the original SAC. This is because both methods are trying to reduce the estimation bias to reduce overestimation, which induces an underestimation.

Global Terrorism during 1970-2017

Final Project for CSCI-SHU 235 Information Visualization

Year:

2000

To appeal for wider attention on such heinous action and contempt for life, Yirong Mao and I propose a visualization system, which helps the users to gain a more comprehensive view of the terrorism attacks happened during 1970-2017, as well as provides the functionalities to view accurate number and make comparisons across different countries.

The world map has two views, and the users can transform from these two views by the "change view" button and select a specific year from 1970 to 2017 using the slider. One view is that different countries will be painted with different color saturations from the red color palette according to the amount of terrorist attacks of that country in that year. The redder, the more terrorist attacks of that country, and vice versa. Most countries barely have terrorism attacks, but a few countries have a couple of thousands in amount. So we use a logarithmic color scale for better readability. When the user hovers over the region of a country, it will show a tooltip with that country’s name and the number of terrorist attacks that year. The stroke will also become red for better identification when hovered. The other view is a world map with a single background color. Those cities where terrorist attacks happened will be marked by the point marks. The position of point will show the attack’s location, the color saturation of point will show the number of people killed during that attack. When the user hovers over the point, it will also show the city name, and accurate number of people being killed.

On the right, there is a brief introduction of global terrorism. Below the overview, there is a line chart showing the change of countries’ terrorist attack numbers through 1970 to 2017. Users can click the territory of couples of countries from the left part, and each line will show one country’s trend of terrorist attack numbers. After being clicked and chosen, the stroke of the country will change to green. In the line chart, there is a legend about the color hue and corresponding country. Users can compare different countries’ changes in one view. Users can empty the lines by clicking the "clear" button and choose new ones. Additionally, hovering on the line will also show the country name.
